What is Blockchain?

Blockchain technology is revolutionizing various industries by ensuring safer, more transparent transactions without the need for intermediaries like banks. In this article, we’ll explore the basics of blockchain, how it operates, and how it’s being applied in real-world scenarios.

At its core, blockchain uses cryptography and decentralized networks to secure data, ensuring that records cannot be tampered with. It’s commonly used in cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin, but blockchain's potential extends far beyond that. It’s being implemented in areas like supply chain management, digital identities, and even smart contracts.

The future of blockchain holds great promise, allowing people to make secure, peer-to-peer transactions without relying on a third party, such as a bank. This decentralized approach could bring more control and security to digital interactions across various sectors.
